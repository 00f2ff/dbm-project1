The Recession's Effect On Americans' Activity Engagement

Duncan McIsaac, Luo Lorraine, Jina Lee and Mo Zhang

This project analyzes how Americans altered their schedules and behavior during The Great Recession. We used the American Time Use Survey (ATUS) as our data source. The American Time Use Survey conducted over 148,000 interviews between 2003 and 2013 to keep track of how much time Americans fifteen years of age and older dedicated to a myriad of different activities.
